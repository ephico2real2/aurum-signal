## §4 Industry research findings

Per skill mandate, every composite proposal here cites canonical patterns from MQL5 community + Smart-Money / ICT literature.

### Pattern A — Asian session breakout / range expansion (Apr 1 Asian miss)

**Canonical pattern** (verbatim, [Asian Session Gold Trading Strategy / FXM Brand / Medium](https://medium.com/coinmonks/asian-session-gold-breakout-strategy-the-proven-gold-breakout-strategy-that-works-bab80334be9f)):
> "Gold tends to range during the Asian session and then break out with force when liquidity from Europe enters... The Goldmine Strategy waits for a candle body close outside the Asian box before entering, which filters out 'wick grabs' and liquidity sweeps."

Confirmed pattern: filter for **candle body close** (body_pct > 0.5) outside the range, NOT a wick poke. This matches FORGE's existing `m5_body_pct` atom.

**Risk note** (verbatim, [ACY: Asian Session Secrets](https://acy.com/en/market-news/education/market-education-asian-session-usdjpy-volatility-trading-strategy-j-o-20250818-092018/)):
> "False breakouts occur above or below the range, often during the late Asian session (1:00 AM–3:00 AM EST), with these sweeps clearing retail stops and setting up the real move."

Mitigation: pair with `body_pct > 0.5` + `range_expanding=1` + ADX ≥ 25 confirmation.

### Pattern B — Intraday reversal (Apr 2 London cascade miss)

**Canonical pattern** (verbatim, [Mind Math Money: VWAP Strategy](https://www.mindmathmoney.com/articles/vwap-trading-strategy-the-ultimate-guide-to-volume-weighted-average-price-in-tradingview-2025)):
> "When price reaches extreme deviation bands (±2.5 or 3 standard deviations), mean reversion trades back toward VWAP may offer favorable risk-reward setups."

In Apr 2 08:35, the price was −67 pts below VWAP = ~8× ATR deviation — extreme. The opposite direction holds: this signals **trend continuation in the SELL direction**, not mean reversion (because the multi-hour cascade had momentum).

**Verbatim**, [ICT Killzones / TradingRage](https://tradingrage.com/learn/ict-killzone-explained):
> "Liquidity hunts involve watching for false breaks that grab stops, then fade or follow the liquidity path (common near London open)."

The Apr 2 08:35 cascade was a "follow the liquidity path" move after the 08:00 Asian-high stop hunt. The composite should fire when the body-close confirms the direction.

### Pattern C — Range-bound chop reversion (Apr 2 NY-PM recovery)

**Canonical pattern** ([Volume Profile guide / JournalPlus](https://journalplus.co/strategies/volume-profile-trading/)):
> "The Point of Control (POC) represents the price level with the highest traded volume in the anchored profile and often acts as a key fair value area, support zone, resistance zone, or magnet for price."

Apr 2 16:00 was 18 pts below the daily VWAP and 9 pts below BB-mid — classic "magnet to POC" setup. Pair with confirmed bar-quality (body > 0.5, close > open) + RSI exiting oversold.

### Pattern D — Sustained trend continuation (Apr 1 Asian + LC misses)

**Canonical pattern** ([LiteFinance ORB Strategy](https://www.litefinance.org/blog/for-beginners/trading-strategies/opening-range-breakout-strategy/)):
> "Opening Range Breakout (ORB) is a classic intraday momentum strategy that capitalizes on the initial directional bias established at the start of a trading session... wait for price to break decisively above the range high (bullish breakout) or below the range low (bearish breakout)."

[Capital.com VWAP guide](https://capital.com/en-int/learn/technical-analysis/volume-weighted-average-price-vwap-indicator) — adds the institutional-flow context: VWAP-above + bullish PSAR confirms institutional buy-side flow.

Adapted to FORGE: a TREND_CONTINUATION composite that requires (a) prior 30-min session range broken **by body close**, (b) h1_trend already aligned (avoids range-day false breakouts), (c) M5 ADX > 25 to confirm momentum. This is the §5.2 `TREND_CONTINUATION_BUY` design exactly.

### Pattern E — Killzone awareness (cross-cutting)

[FXNX: ICT Killzones / XAUUSD](https://fxnx.com/en/blog/ict-killzones-master-xauusd-timing-maximum-profit):

### Composite C — `RANGE_REVERSION_BUY` (Apr 2 NY-PM 16:00 recovery + chop-day captures)

**Layer/role**: Setup. Targets RANGE / chop regimes where price has just touched a session-low extreme. Distinct from BB_LOWER_REVERSION_BUY (which is BB-band based) — this is **session-anchor based** (uses iLow(D1,0), VWAP, POC).

**Trigger condition**: Price at/near session low + RSI exiting oversold + bar quality positive + macro NOT actively bearish.

**6 atoms**:

| # | Atom | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | `(price - iLow(_Symbol,PERIOD_D1,0)) / m5_atr` | ≤ 0.5 | Within 0.5×ATR of day low — at the structural anchor |
| Q2 | `m5_rsi` | ≥ 30 | Exiting oversold (was sub-30 within last 6 bars) |
| Q3 | `m5_rsi > iRSI(..., shift=1)` AND `m5_rsi > iRSI(..., shift=3)` | true | RSI rising (not falling) |
| Q4 | `m5_close > m5_open` | true | Bullish bar |
| Q5 | `m5_body_pct` | ≥ 0.5 | Strong bar (filters dojis at the bottom — they're indecision, not reversal) |
| Q6 | `h1_trend_strength` | ≥ -0.3 | Macro NOT actively bearish (prevents BUY into trending bear day) |

**Threshold**: **5/6 required**. Composite is direction-explicit (BUY only).

**Mirror — `RANGE_REVERSION_SELL`**:
| # | Atom | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | `(iHigh(_Symbol,PERIOD_D1,0) - price) / m5_atr` | ≤ 0.5 (within 0.5×ATR of day high) |
| Q2 | `m5_rsi` | ≤ 70 (exiting overbought) |
| Q3 | RSI declining | RSI < t-1 AND t-3 |
| Q4 | `m5_close < m5_open` | true |
| Q5 | `m5_body_pct` | ≥ 0.5 |
| Q6 | `h1_trend_strength` | ≤ +0.3 |

## §6 Validation plan

**Phase 1 — Logged-only validation** (zero risk):
1. Add the 3 composites with `FORGE_SETUP_*_ENABLED=0` so they don't actually take trades.
2. Add **shadow logging**: when atoms pass, emit a SIGNAL row with `setup_type='<COMPOSITE>'`, `outcome='SHADOW_TAKE'`, and the composite atom snapshot. New outcome value extension needed in the SIGNALS schema (additive; non-breaking).
3. Re-run a multi-day backtest covering Mar 31 → Apr 8 (same period as this case study). Confirm:
   - `TREND_CONTINUATION_BUY` fires at Apr 1 03:00 (Miss #3), Apr 1 18:00 (Miss #4), Mar 31 18:00 (Miss #5).
   - `INTRADAY_REVERSAL_SELL` fires at Apr 2 08:35 (Miss #1), Apr 2 09:00.
   - `RANGE_REVERSION_BUY` fires at Apr 2 16:15 (Miss #2 refined).
   - **NONE** of them fire at G5006/G5048/Apr-8-04:10-knife-catch coordinates.

**Phase 2 — Live but capped** (low risk):
1. Enable `FORGE_SETUP_TREND_CONTINUATION_ENABLED=1` first (most defensible — 4/9 misses, lowest false-positive risk).
2. Lot factor capped at 0.5× base for first 100 fills.
3. Monitor for 1 week: count entries, win rate, average P&L per entry.

**Phase 3 — Full deployment** (operator approval gated):
- If Phase 2 win-rate ≥ 60% on ≥20 entries, lift lot cap to 1.0×.
- Enable INTRADAY_REVERSAL_SELL and RANGE_REVERSION_BUY in sequence with same Phase-2 gating.

**Validation data needed**:
- Phase 1 requires v2.7.36 logging extension (Layer 2 atoms — h1_di_balance, h4_trend logged but POC/Fib in case study §4b are now in `SIGNALS` per atlas §11).
- Phase 1 requires the `SHADOW_TAKE` outcome value or equivalent (`outcome='WOULD_TAKE'` keyword). Implementation cost: 1 line in `JournalRecordSignal`.

**Refute conditions** (what would kill the composite):
- TREND_CONTINUATION_BUY fires ≥ 1 time at G5006-equivalent coords → ADX threshold too lenient → raise from 25 to 30.
- INTRADAY_REVERSAL_SELL fires ≥ 1 time during a true range day (h1_trend < 0.3 entire window) → atom R1 threshold too lenient.
- RANGE_REVERSION_BUY fires on a trending-bear day where h1 < -0.5 → atom Q6 threshold too lenient (raise to 0.0 or +0.3).
